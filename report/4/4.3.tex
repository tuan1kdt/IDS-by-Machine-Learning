\subsubsection*{Key Observations.}
\textbf{LightGBM with SMOTE} achieves the highest Macro-$F_1$ (0.9241) and Balanced Accuracy (0.9253), making it the best overall model for detecting minority attack classes equitably.
\textbf{XGBoost} is a close second and has the highest MCC (0.9947), indicating near-perfect ranking across all confusion-matrix cells.
\textbf{Random Forest} provides a solid baseline (Macro-$F_1$ = 0.9202 before SMOTE) with the shortest training time among ensemble methods (62 s), making it the most practical choice when compute is constrained.
\textbf{Decision Tree}, while achieving 90.7\% Macro-$F_1$, shows that even a simple non-ensemble learner captures most of the discriminative signal in the CIC-IDS2017 flow features.

All four models comfortably exceed the 0.95 Macro-$F_1$ target for the dominant, well-represented attack classes; the overall Macro-$F_1$ falls below 0.95 because of the three genuinely hard classes (\texttt{Web Attack -- XSS}, \texttt{Web Attack -- Brute Force}, and \texttt{Rare\_Attack}), which are discussed in detail in Section~\ref{sec:exp-per-class}.
