\subsection{Runtime Characteristics}
\label{sec:exp-latency}

Table~\ref{tab:runtime} summarises the training time and estimated per-flow inference latency for each model, measured on the Colab CPU runtime.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Training time and inference cost per model.}
\label{tab:runtime}
\begin{tabular}{lrrr}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{Train time (s)} & \textbf{Serialised size (MB)} & \textbf{Batch inference (flows/s)} \\
\midrule
LightGBM      & 212 & $\approx$ 5   & $> 50{,}000$ \\
XGBoost       & 157 & $\approx$ 8   & $> 40{,}000$ \\
Random Forest &  62 & $\approx$ 150 & $> 30{,}000$ \\
Decision Tree &   7 & $<$ 1         & $> 200{,}000$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\noindent All tree-based models comfortably satisfy the $10^4$ flows per second throughput target and the $< 512$ MB memory budget defined in Section~\ref{sec:problem-statement}. The Random Forest serialised model is the heaviest at $\approx 150$ MB (200 full-depth trees), but it remains well within the memory budget. LightGBM and XGBoost are both compact ($< 10$ MB), making them the preferred choice for resource-constrained deployments.

The training time of LightGBM (212 s with SMOTE) is slightly higher than XGBoost (157 s) because of the additional histogram-construction overhead for the augmented training set. However, this cost is incurred only once offline; it does not affect the online inference latency budget.
