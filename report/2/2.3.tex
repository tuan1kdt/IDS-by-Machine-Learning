\subsection{Data Preprocessing}
\label{sec:bg-preproc}

Raw flow-level records cannot be consumed directly by most learning algorithms. We address four preprocessing concerns: data cleaning, normalisation, label encoding, and class-imbalance handling.

\subsubsection*{Data Cleaning.}
We drop rows containing \texttt{NaN} or $\pm\infty$ values, remove exact duplicate flows, and eliminate zero-variance columns. After cleaning, approximately 99.6\% of the original 2.83M records remain.

\subsubsection*{Normalisation and Label Encoding.}
CIC-IDS2017 features span many orders of magnitude. Tree-based learners (Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM) are invariant to monotonic rescaling and require only that the scaler is fitted on the training split and applied frozen to test data. We use \texttt{StandardScaler} (Z-score normalisation) as the default. Categorical labels are encoded as integers via scikit-learn's \texttt{LabelEncoder} fitted on training labels only.

\subsubsection*{Class-Imbalance Handling.}
CIC-IDS2017 is dominated by benign traffic ($\approx 80\%$); naively trained models collapse to the trivial \texttt{BENIGN} classifier. Two complementary strategies are combined in our pipeline:

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.2em,itemsep=2pt,topsep=2pt]
    \item[Cost-sensitive weighting.] All models are trained with \texttt{class\_weight='balanced'}, which re-weights each training example by the inverse frequency of its class. This is cheap and requires no data alteration.
    \item[SMOTE oversampling~\cite{ref_smote}.] Minority classes are augmented with synthetic examples generated by interpolating between a minority-class sample and one of its $k$ nearest neighbours in feature space:
    \begin{equation}
        \mathbf{x}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{x}_i + \lambda \cdot (\mathbf{x}_{j} - \mathbf{x}_i), \quad \lambda \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1).
    \end{equation}
    We apply Proportional SMOTE (Section~\ref{sec:exp-setup}) \emph{only to the training split}, so that reported test metrics reflect the unaugmented operating distribution.
\end{description}
